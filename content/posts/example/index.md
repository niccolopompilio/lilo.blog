Human beings have been roaming this planet for hundreds of thousands of years. We’ve built thriving societies and booming economies. We’ve learned almost everything about ourselves and our surroundings, but there is still one topic which baffles me. Though I’ve never actually been in love, I still find the phenomenon itself highly intriguing. Love or being loved is something that many people crave and only few are blessed with. Bob Dylan once said, "You can never be wise and in love at the same time.". I think this quote is very interesting because even though it reflects on one man’s perception and understanding of what it means to be in love, the people who have read it agree and relate to it. I would like to break this quote up into two parts: How the brain interprets being in love and how the heart does the same.

The human brain is one of the most powerful assets a person possesses, but when it’s met with the challenge of love most of its decisions revolve around just that. It becomes completely irrational and focuses mainly on the subject of its desire. As someone who tries to be more rational than emotional, I can fully relate to these types of struggles. When I’m faced with a dilemma where my heart wants me to do one thing, but my brain wants me to do the other, I try to act reasonably and listen to my brain. This has however proven to be difficult in the past. Therefore, the brain tries to do the more logical thing when it comes to love and not be too overbearing.

The heart on the other hand, while not actually having the ability to make its own decisions, is usually associated with love and all its other facets. When someone says that their head is telling them to do one thing, but their heart is telling them to do another, they’re referring to this phenomenon. Even though both impulse signals are being sent from the brain, the one to do more with love is linked with the heart. These decisions are usually the ones that make a person feel excited and like they have butterflies in their stomach. In the past I’ve found that while listening to my heart in the moment may feel good, the end result has typically been proven to be rather unpleasant.

Even though I myself have never been in love, I understand that it’s a very overwhelming and complex feeling. It can make people act in strange and unexpected ways. While the brain allows someone to act responsibly, the heart almost forces them to act more irrationally. Both of these qualities are important to allow a person to function properly and prosper. Ultimately, I agree with Bob Dylan’s statement in that it is difficult to be wise when you are conflicted with feelings of love.
